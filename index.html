No arguing over incidents in the chats. All incident discussions must be handled by the stewards through the proper channels.

2. Race Night and Console Type

We will race crossplay.

2.1. Special Rules for Crossplay Races: Crossplay races follow the same rules as regular races. However, drivers must ensure stable connectivity. If a driver is found to have poor connection repeatedly, they may be moved to the reserve list until the issue is resolved.

3. Attendance

3.1. The cut-off for attendance is 1 hour before the race. The attendance app will not allow drivers to mark or change attendance after this time.

3.1a. If reserves are waiting to race, your seat will be allocated to them. 3.1b. If you want to race after the cut-off and the seat has already been taken, you will be allocated a

reserve seat if available.

3.2. You will not score points in the driver or constructors championship as a reserve.

3.3 If a full-time driver misses a check-in for 2 consecutive weeks or marks attendance and doesn't show up to race, they will lose their seat for that race and be given a reserve role.

4. Qualifying

4.1. During qualifying, you must not use intermediate or wet tires unless in wet/damp conditions. Anyone seen doing so may be given a penalty by stewards.

4.2. No blocking drivers on a flying lap. If someone behind you is faster, stay on your racing line if both drivers are on a flying lap. It is the duty of the driver behind to pass the slower car safely.

5. Five-Minute Break

5.1. After qualifying or the sprint concludes, we will wait on the starting grid for 5 minutes before readying up. This break allows drivers to submit urgent incident reports, take a toilet break, or grab a smoke before the race starts.

5.2. Readying up early will result in a steward penalty.

6. Racing

6.1. Race starts are usually the most hectic in any form of racing. We ask that all drivers approach the first few corners with caution to avoid early incidents or damage.

6.2. Always give space to each driver. Drivers are entitled to space if their front wheel is in line with your rear wheel.

6.3. Under no circumstances is it acceptable to dive bomb a driver when overtaking. If you do this and cause damage to the defending driver, you will be punished if they report the clip to the stewards.

6.4. No intentional crashing into another driver or attempting to DNF another driver. This could result in a full-season ban and possibly removal from the server.

6.5. No continuous swerving on straights if a driver is trying to make a move.

6.6. We want hard but clean racing at all times.

6.7. Be mindful of other drivers when rejoining the track. Make sure you do so safely.

6.8. Resetting to the track is highly prohibited and will result in steward penalties.

7. Reserve Drivers

7.1. Reserve drivers must have marked attendance using the reserve driver icon to be invited. Seats will be allocated by staff.

7.2. Reserve drivers can qualify.

7.3. Reserve drivers do not score points in drivers or constructors championships.

7.4. Reserve drivers will be treated like a full-time driver regarding penalties issued by the Stewards.

8. Safety Car

8.1. Nobody likes to see a safety car unless you screwed up your first corners and took damage. Safety car restarts cause many problems as drivers are eager to get racing again. Every driver must keep their front tire behind the rear wheel of the driver in front upon race restart until the track goes green.

8.2. Please maintain a 2-car gap to the car in front in case they have to slow down to reduce their deltas.

8.3. Safety Car AI can be used to have/get a drink, quick toilet break, or add someone back into the game. However, if your AI unfairly gains a position, you should give it back, and if people are seen abusing this, they could receive penalty points (PP). We wouldn't expect anyone to be on AI for more than 1 lap under SC, and you are fully responsible for your AI (due to current issues with tyres warming up, use of AI under SC is strictly prohibited).

8.4. There is no overtaking during the safety car unless the car in front goes off track.

9. Incident Reporting

9.1. We understand people get annoyed if they DNF or take damage during a race; we all do. After qualifying/sprint concludes, we will wait on the starting grid for up to 5 minutes to allow drivers, if they wish, the opportunity to submit any incident reports that need to be urgently dealt with by stewards.

9.2. Once the race has been completed, drivers have until 7 pm the following day to submit any reports to the stewards. It is the driver's responsibility to submit their POV footage when requested by the stewards in a ticket. Failure to provide the footage will result in the following penalties.

First Time: Warning

Second Time: 1 penalty point

12. License Point System

12.1. Stewards will follow a penalty point system for each reported incident. 12.1a. If a driver is at fault, they will receive penalty points.

12.2. Penalty Points Thresholds:

3 Points: Time penalty for the next driver's race (5 seconds).

5 Points: Position drop for the next driver's race (3 positions).

8 Points: Qualification ban.

15 Points: Race ban.

20 Points: Driver championship points drop (5 points).

25 Points: Driver championship points drop (10 points).

30 Points: Season ban.

35 Points: League ban.

12.3. Penalty Points Drop Policy:

A driver may drop 1 penalty point after 3 consecutive clean races.

A "clean race" is defined as one where no steward penalties are issued.

12.4. Warnings Policy:

Drivers will receive a penalty point after accumulating 3 warnings.

One penalty point is applied for each set of 3 warnings.

Warnings are cleared when all penalty points have been dropped.

12.5. Accumulation of points will result in a qualifying ban, race ban, season ban, or removal from the league based on the thresholds outlined above.
